Background & Family:
I was born in Columbus, Ohio on June 12, 1979.  When I was about 4 years old, my family moved to Amanda, Ohio, a small farming community about an hour southeast of Columbus. We own about 12 acres of land where my Dad has built ( and is still working on ) a  beautiful two story house with the help of my mom, brothers, and myself.  We are also currently working on a bachelor pad/ workshop/ workout place/ office. 

My dad was originally a carpenter in the carpenter's union, but became a firefighter when I was 5.  He is currently a Battalion Chief for the Columbus Div. of Fire.  My Mom is a homemaker.  I have three brothers: Mike, Jon, and Tim.  Mike is my identical twin, Jon is 1¼ years younger than me, and Tim is 2½ years younger than me.

Schooling
I attended Amanda Clearcreek High School for all 12 years of my pre-college schooling.  There I was involved in such activities as Fellowship of Christian Athletes, National Honor Society, and numerous other activities. I graduated as co-valedictorian (with my twin, and 2 others) in 1997. 

After graduating, I went to Otterbein College for 4 years.  I majored in accounting and got by Bachelors of Science.  I graduated Summa Cum Laude with a 3.975 GPA. 

I then went on to Capital University where I received my Masters of Business Administration.  I needed this 5th year of schooling so that I could sit for the CPA exam in November of 2002.

 Sports
I love to play sports.  I have played football, basketball, little league baseball, church softball, and ran track. I enjoy playing most other sports too, including tennis, racquetball, volleyball, hockey, and others.


Football
 I  played football from 7th grade until my sophomore year in college. I  lettered two years as cornerback and wide receiver in high school for my team, the Aces; and I lettered one year as cornerback for the Otterbein College Cardinals.   In high school, my team made it to the playoffs all four years.  I actually got to play in the playoffs when I was a junior and senior.  My junior year we went undefeated, but were defeated in the reional final game.  My senior year we were once again undefeated in the regular season, but got beat in the state semi-final game.  I decided to quit playing football as a junior in college because it took much time, I didn't care for the coaching, and wasn't as much fun as it used to be.

Track
  I started running track when I was in 8th grade, and have run ever since.  I have ran in every running event there is in high school.  My least favorites were the 3200 and the 4x1600 relay (at relay meets).  My most best races were the 110 high (39") and 300 intermediate (36") hurdles and the 4x400 relay.  My junior year I made it to the regionals in the 300 intermediate hurdles, but that is where my season ended. 

My senior year I was more successful.  I broke the school record in the 300 meter hurdles at our very first meet and consistently broke my own record as the season progressed.  I made it to the regional meet in the 300 & 110 hurdles, the 4x400, and the 4x800.  Here I set the current school record of 39.6 in the 300 hurdles.  At state, I ended up 7th in the hurdles and 8th in the 4x400.  I was disappointed that I didn't even get a ribbon or get to stand on the podium, but I was happy to have even made it to the state finals in two events.

I ran track for Otterbein.  Indoor I usually ran the 500m, the 50m high hurdles, and sometimes the 400m or the  4x400.  Outdoor I mainly ran the 110 high (42") hurdles, 400 intermediate (36") hurdles (54.2 sec.),
threw javelin (160 ft) and sometimes ran the 400 (51) or 4x400 relay.
